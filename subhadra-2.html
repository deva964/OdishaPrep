Odisha Govt Launches Subhadra 2.0 Scheme

Published: Feb 27, 2026 | OdishaPrep News Desk

The Government of Odisha has officially launched the **Subhadra 2.0 welfare scheme**, announcing an additional financial assistance of ₹10,000 for eligible women beneficiaries across the state. Chief Minister Mohan Majhi introduced the upgraded program during a public event in Bhubaneswar.

What is Subhadra 2.0 Scheme?

Subhadra 2.0 is an enhanced version of the earlier women welfare initiative aimed at improving financial independence among women belonging to economically weaker sections. The scheme focuses on direct cash transfers, skill development, and self-employment opportunities.

According to government officials, nearly **1.2 crore women** are expected to benefit from this phase. Funds will be credited directly to beneficiaries' bank accounts through the DBT (Direct Benefit Transfer) system.

Key Highlights

  • ₹10,000 additional financial assistance
  • Direct Bank Transfer (DBT)
  • Coverage expanded across rural districts
  • Special focus on SHG women groups

Government Statement

Chief Minister Mohan Majhi stated that the objective of the scheme is to strengthen women's participation in Odisha’s economic growth. He emphasized that empowering women financially will accelerate rural development and social stability.

Impact on Odisha Economy

Experts believe the scheme will increase household spending, improve education access for children, and encourage entrepreneurship among women. Analysts also expect a positive impact on small businesses and local markets.

How to Apply

Eligible beneficiaries can apply through local Panchayat offices or the official Odisha government welfare portal. Verification will be completed through Aadhaar and bank linkage.
